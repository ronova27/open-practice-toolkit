# Product Vision

OpenPractice Toolkit exists to make everyday music practice tools accessible, inspectable, and useful without subscription barriers.

## Audience

- Students building rhythm and listening skills.
- Pianists who want to prehear notes or chords before practice.
- Percussionists and taiko players working on timing and motion.
- Teachers creating simple practice material for learners.
- Self-learners who want clear feedback without needing a full digital audio workstation.

## Product Principles

- Useful before fancy.
- Beginner-friendly without hiding important musical concepts.
- Local-first where feasible.
- Privacy-respecting by default.
- Open formats over lock-in.
- No subscription requirement for core practice features.
- Clear limits around transcription and experimental analysis.

## What The MVP Should Prove

- A browser can provide a reliable and understandable advanced metronome.
- Learners can enter simple notation and hear it before practicing.
- Timing feedback can be shown in a way that helps rather than shames.
- Approximate transcription is useful when paired with manual correction.
- Experimental video timing can support rhythm research without pretending to be magic.

## Non-Goals For The Founding Phase

- A full notation editor.
- Professional DAW replacement.
- Perfect polyphonic transcription.
- Required cloud accounts.
- Paid API dependence for core practice.
- Subscription or billing infrastructure.
